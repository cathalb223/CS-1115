Geography

Initially when designing Ethos I had it in my head that it was a future version of earth wherein something happened that nearly wiped out humanity and introduced magic into the world. In doing so I researched into predicted models for the future configuration of the continents. Once I found one that I liked I modelled it in a map making software called Incarnate.

After creating the map most of my work went into fleshing out and worldbuilding specific locations within the world. I wanted each one to have a unique feel so that I would have room with whatever stories I wanted to set within the world. This lead to a very biome rich world as evidenced by the map above.

Magic System

I found the magic system hardest to think about really. I had to balance needs with aesthetics and theme. I needed to have both divine and arcane magic (magic that comes from a god and magic that is more innate to the world respectively) to tell the stories I wanted to tell. But I also wanted magic to have an unstable and almost accidental quality to it in the setting

Eventually I came up with the idea that whatever introduced magic to the world was a violent and catastrophic event. Many realms crashed into earth and began leaking through. In this model magic is a form of radiation that changes things and people that touch it. Divine magics come from those realms that for one reason or another have developped intelligence.

Themes

The themes of this setting were perhaps the easiest aspect to come up with. I am somewhat of a hack and at the time of worldbuilding I was revisiting a series that I watched as a child on Youtube. While the premise and execution were pretty bad in hindsight, they did deliver in theme and atmosphere. The moody and oppressive decay of a civilisation that is being constantly stalked by things they do not understand. A world that feels like it is on the precipice of becoming worse forever.

While bleak, I found that exploring those concepts was really appealing to me. So I baked them into the setting as best as I could.
